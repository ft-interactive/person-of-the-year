FT Person of the Year

2016: the winner

2016

Donald Trump

President-elect of the United States

Donald Trump did not upend US democracy on November 8, 2016. It has been dysfunctional for years. It is no coincidence that Mr Trump’s victory took place in the same year as Brexit, the No vote in the Italian referendum and other populist eruptions across the western world.

The runners up

2016

Omran Daqneesh

Resident of Aleppo

For a brief moment on August 17, 2016, five-year-old Omran Daqneesh reminded the world of its own numbness to the tragedy ripping apart Syria’s ancient city of Aleppo - and a war that has shaken the Middle East and sent its shock waves across the world.

Full profile

2016

Nigel Farage

Former UK Independence party leader

Nigel Farage became the face of a populist movement sweeping Europe by laying the ground for the Brexit victory before he was co-opted by Donald Trump, who told cheering rallies that he would “do a Brexit” and seize the keys to the White House.

Full profile

2016

Narendra Modi

Indian prime minister

On November 8, Narenda Modi declared an immediate ban on the use of Rs500 and Rs1,000 notes – 86% percent of India’s circulating cash supply in a highly cash-driven economy. Indians are now grappling with an unprecedented and highly-disruptive experiment, whose outcome will be studied by economists for years to come.

Full profile

2016

Angela Merkel

German chancellor

For Angela Merkel, 2016 was another dramatic year which put her centre-stage in global affairs and left her facing calls to take over outgoing US president Barack Obama’s mantle as the leader of the western world.

Full profile

2016

Masayoshi Son

Founder and CEO of Softbank

Less than a month after Britain voted to leave the EU, Masayoshi Son, the risk-addicted billionaire who built Japan’s SoftBank into a global internet powerhouse, clinched a deal to acquire UK chip designer Arm Holdings for £24.3bn. But this audacious deal was only the first for Mr Son.
